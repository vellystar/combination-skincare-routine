✦ UNDERSTANDING YOUR SKIN ✦
🔬

What Is Combination Skin?

Combination skin is defined by having two or more different skin types coexisting on the face at the same time. Unlike oily skin (oily everywhere) or dry skin (dry everywhere), combination skin is characterised by:

  • An oily T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) caused by a higher density of sebaceous (oil) glands in that area producing excess sebum.
  • Normal to dry cheeks that may feel tight, flaky, or rough — especially after cleansing or in cool weather.
  • Pores that appear visibly enlarged on the nose and central forehead, but fine or invisible on the cheeks.
  • A tendency toward shine in the T-zone by midday while cheeks remain matte or even feel uncomfortable.
🧬

Why Do You Have Combination Skin?

Combination skin is largely genetic — it's the skin type you were born with. However, several factors influence how pronounced the differences between zones are:

  • Genetics & hormones: Androgens stimulate sebum production, and the T-zone simply has more sebaceous glands — this is biological.
  • Climate & humidity: Hot, humid weather amplifies T-zone oiliness; dry, cold air can worsen cheek dryness.
  • Over-cleansing: Using harsh cleansers strips the entire face, causing the T-zone to overcompensate with even more oil.
  • Skincare mismatch: Products too rich for the T-zone or too lightweight for the cheeks can exaggerate the imbalance.
  • Diet & stress: High glycaemic index foods and stress hormones can amplify oiliness in the T-zone.
✨

Signs You Have Combination Skin

You likely have combination skin if you recognise these experiences:

  • Your T-zone looks shiny or greasy by midday but your cheeks do not.
  • You get blackheads or clogged pores mainly on and around the nose.
  • Your cheeks feel tight, slightly rough, or flaky in cold weather or after cleansing.
  • Finding moisturisers is hard — lighter ones feel insufficient on cheeks; richer ones feel greasy on the T-zone.
  • Breakouts appear mainly on the forehead, nose, and chin — rarely on the cheeks.
  • Makeup tends to slide off the nose and forehead but clings or looks patchy on the cheeks.
💜

The Core Skincare Philosophy

The golden rule for combination skin is balance without extremes. Your routine should:

  • Cleanse gently — never strip. A low-pH, gentle cleanser preserves the acid mantle across all zones.
  • Hydrate everywhere — even oily skin is dehydrated. When the T-zone is dehydrated, it produces even more oil to compensate. Lightweight hydration is essential for the whole face.
  • Multi-mask or spot-treat where possible — use mattifying treatments on the T-zone and richer ingredients on the cheeks when needed.
  • Protect with SPF daily — UV damage worsens sebum production and speeds up skin ageing across all zones equally.
  • Use non-comedogenic products — ingredients that won't clog pores are key, especially for the T-zone.
🌤️

How Your Skin Changes Seasonally

Combination skin is especially sensitive to seasonal shifts — adapt your routine accordingly:

  • Hot & humid (wet season): T-zone oiliness intensifies. Use gel moisturisers, blotting papers, and lightweight layers. Double-cleanse more diligently.
  • Hot & dry (dry season / AC-heavy environments): Cheeks may become dehydrated and flaky while the T-zone remains oily. Add a hydrating serum or toner layer for the cheeks.
  • Cold weather: Sebum production may decrease slightly overall, but cheeks can become very dry. You may need a richer moisturiser seasonally.
  • Indoors with air conditioning: AC strips moisture from the air and from your skin — misting and extra hydration layers help maintain comfort.
🎯

Key Ingredients for Combination Skin

These hero ingredients are ideal for combination skin and are present across your current routine:

  • Niacinamide — regulates sebum in the T-zone, minimises pores, and brightens tone everywhere.
  • Hyaluronic Acid — provides lightweight hydration that doesn't trigger oil production.
  • Centella Asiatica — soothes redness and repairs the barrier without adding heaviness.
  • Ceramides — rebuild the skin barrier on dry cheek areas while being non-comedogenic for oily zones.
  • Low-pH cleansers — preserve the skin's natural acid mantle and microbiome across all zones.
  • Snail Secretion Filtrate — repairs micro-damage, hydrates, and balances without greasiness.
✦ DO'S & DON'TS ✦

✓   DO These

  • Use a gentle, pH-balanced cleanser — no stripping formulas.
  • Apply lightweight hydration all over — including the T-zone.
  • Use gel or water-cream textures in the morning for the whole face.
  • Blot excess oil mid-day instead of skipping moisturiser.
  • Apply SPF every single day, year-round.
  • Focus richer, more nourishing products on the cheeks specifically in the PM.
  • Use non-comedogenic products to avoid clogging T-zone pores.
  • Stay consistent — combination skin responds best to routine stability.
  • Hydrate from the inside — drink adequate water daily.

✗   AVOID These

  • Over-cleansing or using harsh foaming cleansers — worsens T-zone oiliness.
  • Skipping moisturiser on the T-zone because it's "already oily."
  • Using heavy, occlusive creams all over — clogs pores in the T-zone.
  • Applying toners with alcohol — strips the barrier and triggers rebound oiliness.
  • Touching your face throughout the day — transfers bacteria and oil.
  • Using physical exfoliants too aggressively — causes micro-tears and irritation.
  • Skipping sunscreen on cloudy days or when indoors — UV still penetrates.
  • Trying to "dry out" the T-zone — this backfires and increases oil production.
  • Switching products too frequently — give each product 4–6 weeks to show results.
💡

Remember: Combination skin is not a problem to fix — it's a skin type to understand and work with. Morning Skincare Ritual

Prepare, protect & prime your skin for the day ahead ✦

☀️   A.M. Routine · ~10 min
💜

Combination skin tip: In the morning, keep it light — focus on hydration without overloading the skin. Save heavier treatments for the evening. Always finish with SPF — rain or shine, indoors or out!

1
Gentle Cleanse
Somethinc Low PH Gentle Cleanser or Pond's Biome Gel Cera-Hyamino

Wet face with lukewarm water — hot water disrupts your skin barrier. Take a pea-sized amount and gently massage in small circular motions for 60 seconds, focusing on the T-zone (forehead, nose, chin). Rinse thoroughly and gently pat dry with a clean towel — never rub. Choose Pond's Biome on days when skin feels tight or sensitive; Choose Somethinc on days when skin feels oilier.

Pour a small amount (5–7 drops) onto your palms or a reusable cotton pad. If using palms: gently press and pat into skin — do not rub. Press into cheeks, forehead, chin. Focus extra attention on dry cheek areas by pressing repeatedly until absorbed. Let absorb for 30–60 seconds before moving on.

Centella Ampoule
SKIN1004 Madagascar Centella Ampoule

Why? A lightweight, soothing serum that calms redness, soothes any inflammation, and reinforces your skin barrier — perfect for combination skin prone to occasional sensitivity.

Apply generously — the "2 finger rule": squeeze sunscreen across two fingers from base to tip for face and neck coverage. Apply as the very last skincare step, at least 1 minute after moisturizer. Evening Recovery Ritual

Repair, renew & restore while you sleep ✦

🌙   P.M. Routine · ~15 min
💙

Evening philosophy: Nighttime is when your skin does its heaviest repair work. This is the time for your richer, more active products. Don't rush — take your time with each step so your skin can fully absorb and benefit. Double cleansing is a must if you wore makeup or sunscreen.

1
Double Cleanse
First Cleanse (Micellar/Oil) → Somethinc Low PH or Pond's Biome
›
Why Double Cleanse? Sunscreen and makeup are oil-based and won't fully dissolve with a water-based cleanser alone. A two-step cleanse ensures a truly clean canvas for your actives.
First Cleanse: Use a micellar water, cleansing balm, or oil cleanser. Apply to dry skin, massage gently to dissolve SPF and makeup. Rinse with warm water. Second Cleanse (Somethinc Low pH): Now use your water-based cleanser on damp skin to remove any remaining residue. Massage 60 seconds, rinse thoroughly. No makeup day? Skip to single cleanse with Pond's Biome Gel — its ceramide and hyaluronic acid formula is extra gentle for bare skin. Pat dry with a fresh, clean towel. Your skin should feel clean but not tight.
2
Repair Toner
SOME BY MI Snail Truecica Miracle Repair Toner
›
Why? After cleansing, your skin is slightly dehydrated. This toner instantly replenishes hydration, soothes any post-cleanse sensitivity, and preps your skin to absorb the ampoule and cream more effectively.
Apply a slightly more generous amount than in the morning — evening skin can be slightly more receptive. Use the 7-skin method on especially dry nights: apply 3–5 thin layers one by one, letting each absorb before the next. Gently pat — the snail secretion and truecica begin working to repair micro-damage from the day.

✦ SLEEP & REPAIR ✦
🌙

Sleep hygiene for better skin: Sleep on a clean, silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction. Aim for 7–8 hours — growth hormone peaks during deep sleep, which is prime skin repair time. Keep a humidifier nearby for overnight hydration boost!

Mid-Day Refresh & Reapply

Keep your skin protected and your look fresh throughout the day ✦

✨   Reapply every 2 hrs outdoors
☀️

Why reapply? Sunscreen breaks down from UV exposure, sweat, sebum, and rubbing. For proper protection, reapply every 2 hours outdoors and after swimming or sweating. When wearing makeup, cushion foundations with SPF can double as convenient reapplication tools!

🌞 SPF Reapplication

Maintaining SPF protection throughout the day is just as important as the initial application. Here's how to reapply over bare skin or makeup:

1
Bare skin day: Simply reapply YOU Physical Sunscreen Sunbrella directly — use the 2-finger rule amount, press gently into skin.
2
Over makeup: The cleanest method is to use a cushion foundation with SPF (TIRTIR or SKINTIFIC) — gently press, don't drag, for a top-up that refreshes coverage and adds SPF.
3
Alternatively, use a setting spray with SPF or a mineral powder SPF over makeup for the least disruptive reapplication.
4
If spending extended time indoors (office, WFH), reapply every 4 hours minimum — UV rays still penetrate windows.

💄 Makeup Touch-Up

Refresh your look without disturbing your base by following this smart touch-up sequence:

1
Blot first: Use oil blotting paper on the T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) to remove excess sebum before adding any product.
2
Cushion touch-up: Use TIRTIR Mask Fit Red Cushion or SKINTIFIC Cushion — press lightly only where coverage has faded (typically center of face, around nose).
3
Set with powder: A light dusting of Second Date Translucent Loose Powder on T-zone absorbs any lingering shine and sets your touch-up for hours.
4
Blusher refresh: A light dab of blush on the cheekbones instantly revives a tired-looking face — keep your blush compact in your bag!

💧 Skin Refresh Spritz

Spraying hydrating mist mid-day gives your skin a moisture boost and makes makeup look fresh again — without disturbing it.

1
Hold a facial mist (centella water, rosewater, or hyaluronic acid mist) at arm's length from your face.
2
Mist in a figure-8 motion across the face, eyes closed. Let it absorb — don't rub. This refreshes both skin and makeup.
3
The SKIN1004 Centella Water is an excellent choice to pair with your existing routine — consistent with your centella skincare system.

TIRTIR or SKINTIFIC Cushion — for SPF + coverage touch-up in one step.
