My Combination Skin

Understanding your unique skin type is the foundation of every effective skincare routine. Here's everything you need to know about combination skin and how it behaves.

What is Combination Skin?

Combination skin features both oily and dry zones simultaneously. The T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) tends to be oily while cheeks are normal to dry — requiring a balanced, multi-zone approach to every routine.

🧬

How It Works

Sebaceous glands are hyperactive along the T-zone, producing excess sebum. Cheeks have fewer active glands, leading to dryness or dehydration. Hormones, humidity, diet, and stress all shift this balance daily.

🌡️

How It Feels

Forehead and nose feel greasy by midday. Cheeks may feel tight or rough. Pores appear enlarged around the nose. Skin feels balanced right after cleansing but quickly reverts to its zones.

💧

Dehydration vs. Oiliness

Combination skin is often dehydrated — oily zones produce oil to compensate for lack of water moisture. This is why lightweight water-based hydration is key; avoiding moisturizer entirely makes oiliness worse.

🌦️

Indonesian Climate Effect

In humid tropical weather, the T-zone gets significantly oilier. In air-conditioned rooms, cheeks dry out faster. Adjust routine intensity accordingly and always prioritize SPF against intense UV exposure.

✅ What to Look For in Products

  • Low pH cleansers (4.5–5.5) — maintain acid mantle
  • Lightweight, water-based moisturizers
  • Non-comedogenic formulas for T-zone
  • Niacinamide to balance sebum production
  • Ceramides to repair the skin barrier on cheeks
  • Centella asiatica for calming inflammation
  • Gel or gel-cream textures for T-zone areas
  • SPF every single day — non-negotiable

❌ What to Avoid

  • Harsh stripping cleansers (SLS/SLES-heavy)
  • Heavy occlusive creams all over T-zone
  • Alcohol-heavy toners or astringents
  • Over-exfoliating (disrupts the barrier)
  • Skipping moisturizer on oily zones
  • Mixing too many actives without waiting
  • Touching your face frequently
  • Sleeping with makeup on
💡
Pro Tip for Indonesian Climate: Living in a hot, humid environment means your T-zone will produce more sebum. Focus on oil control during the day with a mattifying sunscreen or setting powder on the T-zone, while keeping cheeks hydrated. Always wear SPF — UV rays break down collagen and worsen hyperpigmentation significantly over time.

☀️ Midday Reapplication

Sunscreen loses efficacy after 2 hours of direct sun exposure. Midday reapplication is non-negotiable in Indonesia's intense tropical climate.

⚡
Why Reapply? UV filters chemically break down after ~2 hours of sun exposure. In Indonesia's equatorial climate this is critical — skipping reapplication cancels out morning SPF and allows UV damage to accelerate pigmentation, premature aging, and barrier damage.

☁️ Mostly Indoors

Reapply once around noon with Bening's Spray. A quick 3-second spritz is sufficient. UV still penetrates glass windows and blue light from screens contributes to pigmentation over time.

🌞 Outdoor Activities

Reapply every 2 hours of direct sun exposure. Spray Bening's generously from 15–20 cm in a slow sweep. Press lightly with fingertips. Top with translucent powder to mattify T-zone after.

💦 After Sweating

Pat skin dry gently then reapply sunscreen spray. Spray format is far less disruptive to makeup than cream formulas. Follow with Second Date Translucent Powder for a fresh, set finish.

🎨 Over Full Makeup

Spray Bening's at arm's length, let settle 30 seconds, then press gently with clean beauty sponge — never rub. Finish with translucent setting powder and setting mist to lock everything in.

Shade Matching Tips: Your foundation shade should be matched at the cheek — not the T-zone, which appears more golden/yellow when oily.
