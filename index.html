What is Combination Skin?

Combination skin is a skin type that features both oily and dry areas simultaneously. Typically, the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) tends to be oily and prone to enlarged pores and breakouts, while the cheeks and outer face may feel normal to dry or even tight. This skin type requires a balanced approach — one that controls excess sebum without over-drying the drier areas.

💧

T-Zone (Oily Areas)

Forehead, nose & chin tend to:

  • Produce excess sebum
  • Look shiny by mid-day
  • Have enlarged, visible pores
  • Be prone to blackheads & breakouts
  • Feel greasy hours after cleansing
🌸

Cheeks (Drier Areas)

Outer face & cheeks may feel:

  • Normal to slightly dry or tight
  • Sensitive to harsh ingredients
  • Flaky or rough if not moisturized
  • Comfortable but needing hydration
  • Less prone to acne
⚠️

Common Concerns

  • Uneven texture across zones
  • Balancing oil without drying out
  • Makeup sliding off the T-zone
  • Breakouts in oily areas only
  • Finding products that suit both zones
✅

Key Skin Goals

  • Balance sebum production
  • Maintain hydration without heaviness
  • Keep pores clean & minimized
  • Calm and protect the skin barrier
  • Even, glowy complexion all day
💡

Do's for Combination Skin

  • Use gentle, low-pH cleansers
  • Layer lightweight, water-based products
  • Apply richer moisturizer on dry areas
  • Use gel-based textures on T-zone
  • Never skip SPF!
  • Double cleanse in the evening
🚫

Don'ts for Combination Skin

  • Avoid over-cleansing or harsh scrubs
  • Skip heavy, comedogenic creams
  • Don't skip moisturizer — even oily zones need it
  • Avoid alcohol-heavy toners
  • Don't use the same amount of product everywhere — zone-based application works better

🗺 Zone-Based Application

For combination skin, applying products in targeted zones is key. Use lightweight, mattifying products (like gel moisturizers) on the T-zone, and richer, more nourishing products on the cheeks. You don't need to draw a strict line — just be mindful of how much product you apply where.

🌡 Seasonal Adjustments

Combination skin can shift with the seasons. In hot, humid months (like most days here in Indonesia), your T-zone may get oilier — opt for gel textures and lighter layers. In cooler months, your cheeks may need a bit more moisture. Adjust your routine accordingly and listen to your skin!

✨ Golden Rules for Your Routine

  • Always apply products from thinnest to thickest consistency.
  • Give each product 30–60 seconds to absorb before layering the next.
  • Your skin barrier is everything — don't skip ceramide and hydrating products.
  • Sunscreen is non-negotiable — it's your #1 anti-aging and protection step.
  • Consistency beats perfection. A simple routine done daily beats a complex one done rarely.
  • Patch test any new product before adding it into your full routine.

💄 Makeup Tips for Combination Skin

Combination skin can be tricky with makeup — the T-zone may cause products to slide or oxidize, while drier cheeks might cling to powder and look cakey. The key is good skincare prep, using lightweight bases, and setting strategically with powder only where needed (mainly the T-zone). Always prime your skin properly and layer strategically.

🌙 Evening Routine Tips

Mid-Day Reapplication

Refresh your SPF and touch up your makeup — stay protected and glowy all afternoon.

Why Mid-Day Reapplication Matters

Sunscreen degrades with UV exposure, sweat, and sebum — meaning the SPF protection you applied in the morning is significantly reduced by midday, especially in Indonesia's tropical climate. Reapplying every 2 hours is the gold standard recommendation by dermatologists. For combination skin, touching up over makeup is also important for oil control and a refreshed appearance.

1

Blot Excess Oil First

Blotting Paper or Clean Tissue

Before applying anything, gently blot the T-zone with a blotting paper or tissue to remove excess sebum and oil. Press gently — do not rub, as rubbing smears makeup and irritates skin. This prevents you from piling new product over a greasy base, which can lead to a cakey or patchy appearance.

💡 Tip: Blotting papers are better than tissues as they absorb oil without removing makeup. Keep a pack in your bag!
2

SPF Reapplication — Over Makeup!

Bening's Sunscreen Spray by Dr. Oky Pratama

This is the hero product for mid-day reapplication. The spray sunscreen allows you to reapply UV protection over your makeup without disturbing it. Hold the bottle 20–30cm from your face, close your eyes, and mist evenly over the entire face. Do NOT rub — let it dry naturally on its own. This maintains your makeup while refreshing your sun protection.

⚠️ Important: SPF sprays should still be applied generously — don't just mist lightly and call it done. Spray 2–3 full passes over the face for adequate coverage. Close eyes and mouth while spraying.
3

Cushion Touch-Up (If Needed)

SKINTIFIC Cover Glow Perfect Cushion — or — TIRTIR Mask Fit Red Cushion

After the SPF spray has dried (1–2 minutes), touch up your base coverage with your cushion foundation if needed. The cushion format is perfect for this — the sponge applicator is self-contained and mess-free. Lightly press onto areas where coverage has faded (around the nose, chin, areas where you've blotted). Do not over-apply — less is more for mid-day touch-ups.

✨ Less is more: For touch-ups, press the puff lightly into the cushion pan — you want barely any product on the puff. Build in very thin layers to avoid cakiness over your daytime wear.
4

Set (T-Zone Only)

Second Date Loose Powder — Translucent

If your T-zone is showing shine even after blotting and touching up, a light dusting of the translucent loose powder finishes and resets everything. Focus solely on the T-zone — forehead, nose bridge, and chin. Leave the cheeks untouched or very lightly powdered to keep them looking natural and glowy rather than flat and dry.

💡 Tip: Use a small travel brush or the powder's lid applicator. Tap off excess, then apply with a very light hand. Mid-day powder touch-ups should be almost invisible when done correctly.
5

Re-Set with Setting Mist

PIXY Aqua-Beauty Protecting Mist Matte-Finish — and/or — L'ORÉAL Infallible 3-Second Setting Mist

Finish your mid-day refresh with a setting mist to lock everything back in place and give skin a fresh, just-done appearance. For combination skin mid-day, you have two options: use the PIXY Matte Mist if your T-zone is oily and you want to control shine; use the L'ORÉAL Setting Mist for a more overall refresh and a natural glowy finish; or layer both (PIXY first, then L'ORÉAL) for the best of both worlds.

✨ Spray technique: Hold 20–25cm away from face, close eyes, mist in a sweeping X-and-T motion. Let dry completely — do not blot or touch. The mist does its job in seconds and will leave your makeup looking freshly applied.

🌤 Mid-Day Routine Tips

  • Set an alarm or reminder at noon and again at 2–3pm to reapply your sunscreen spray — SPF degrades fast in tropical heat.
  • Keep your mid-day bag stocked: blotting papers, Bening's SPF spray, cushion foundation, a small travel powder brush, and at least one setting mist.
  • Don't skip the SPF spray step even if your makeup still looks fine — UV damage is invisible but cumulative.
  • If you've been sweating heavily (workout, outdoor activity), you may need to do a more thorough refresh — remove and reapply.
  • The PIXY + L'ORÉAL mist layering trick works great at mid-day too: matte control on the T-zone + fresh glow on the cheeks.
  • Stay hydrated! Drinking water throughout the day helps your skin maintain a plump, glowing appearance.
  • Avoid touching your face during the day — it transfers oil and bacteria, and can disrupt your makeup and skincare layers.

Makeup Routine

Step-by-step base makeup application — from prep to setting, for combination skin.

Before You Start — Skincare Prep is Everything

Makeup always performs better on well-prepped skin. Make sure you've completed your Morning Skincare Routine (cleanser → toner → ampoule → moisturizer → SPF) and waited at least 5 minutes after sunscreen before beginning makeup. This prevents pilling, improves blending, and ensures your makeup lasts longer. Your skincare is the true base — makeup just enhances it.

🌸 Everyday Base Makeup
1

Prep & Prime — BB Concealer

ONLYOU Concealer Brightening & Isolating BB Cream

This multi-tasking product acts as your primer, BB cream, and concealer all in one first step. Apply it to the entire face for light, even coverage and its isolating (primer-like) properties that help your foundation grip and last longer. Then go back and press an extra layer onto targeted areas — under eyes for dark circles, around the nose, on any blemishes or redness you want to conceal. Blend with clean fingertips or a small concealer brush.

✨ Technique: Apply thin overall first (use fingertips — warmth helps blend). Then dab and press extra product onto spots needing more coverage. Do NOT rub — dabbing preserves coverage and avoids streaks.
2

Choose Your Base — Skin Tint or Foundation

Option A: ESQA Minimalist Blurring Serum Skin Tint (Vanilla) — light, natural days
Option B: SKINTIFIC Cover Glow Cushion (Vanilla) — glowy, medium coverage
Option C: TIRTIR Mask Fit Red Cushion (17C Porcelain) — full coverage, long-wear days

Pick your base depending on the day and how much coverage you want:

  • ESQA Skin Tint — for bare skin, minimal makeup, or skincare-first days. Apply with fingertips or damp sponge by pressing and patting into skin. Buildable sheer-to-light coverage.
  • SKINTIFIC Cushion — for a glowy, dewy K-beauty finish with medium coverage. Press the cushion puff with bouncing motions, starting from the center and working outward.
  • TIRTIR Cushion — for full coverage, transfer-resistant wear. Ideal for long days, events, or humid outdoor settings. Same bouncing technique; note it oxidizes slightly so start with a lighter touch.
💡 Layering tip: You can mix bases — ESQA Skin Tint all over as a base, then a dab of TIRTIR cushion only on blemish-prone areas for targeted coverage. This looks the most natural.
3

Set the T-Zone — Loose Powder

Second Date Loose Powder — Translucent

After your base, the T-zone needs to be set with powder to prevent the oilier areas from breaking down your makeup during the day. Use a fluffy brush and apply the translucent powder only to the forehead, nose bridge, and chin. The cheeks should remain untouched — this keeps them looking naturally dewy and fresh while controlling the shiny zones.

⚠️ Key rule: Less is more with powder. Over-powdering is the fastest way to look cakey. One light pass over the T-zone is all you need — you can always add more, but you can't take it away easily.
4

Optional Extras — Blush, Eye Makeup & Lip

Your preferred blush, eyeshadow, eyeliner, mascara, and lip products

Apply any additional makeup steps here — blush, contour, bronzer, eye looks, brows, and lips. For combination skin, cream-based blush applies more naturally over the cheeks, while powder blush sets neatly over powdered areas. Build your eye and lip look as desired. Keep the cheeks minimal to let your glowy skin show through.

✨ Combo skin blush tip: Apply blush with a light hand — cream formulas blend beautifully into the skin tint or ESQA base, while pressed powder blushes work well over the TIRTIR cushion with loose powder set areas.
5

Lock It In — PIXY Matte Setting Mist (T-Zone Focus)

PIXY Aqua-Beauty Protecting Mist Matte-Finish

Once your full makeup look is complete, reach for the PIXY Matte Mist as your first setting spray. This is your oil-control insurance — it locks the T-zone down, controls shine throughout the day, and sets everything in the oiliest zones without disturbing the rest of your look. Hold 20–25cm away, close your eyes, and spray in a gentle sweeping motion covering the whole face (the matte effect is most powerful on the T-zone where sebum is most active).

⏱ Wait time: After spraying, wait 5–8 seconds and let it dry completely before the next step. Do NOT fan your face or blot — let the formula set on its own for best results.
6

Final Finish — L'ORÉAL Infallible Setting Mist

L'ORÉAL Paris Infallible 3-Second Setting Mist

The last step of your makeup routine — and it makes a huge difference. After the PIXY Matte Mist has dried, finish with the L'ORÉAL Infallible Setting Mist overall. This melts all the layers of product together for a seamless, skin-like finish. It adds a healthy, natural glow to the cheeks, prevents any powdery or cakey look, and locks the entire face for all-day wear. Your makeup will look like it's part of your skin.

✨ The combo effect: PIXY Matte (step 5) controls oily zones → L'ORÉAL Infallible (step 6) unifies everything and adds that natural glass-skin radiance. Together they're perfectly suited for combination skin — matte where you need it, glowy where you want it.

💋 Makeup Routine Pro Tips

  • Always complete your skincare and wait 5 minutes after SPF before starting makeup — this is the most important prep step.
  • Apply every product with pressing and patting motions — dragging disrupts the layers beneath and causes patchiness.
  • The two setting mists are a game-changer for combination skin: PIXY for matte oil control, L'ORÉAL for skin-like finish. Use both every time for maximum wear.
  • If your TIRTIR cushion looks too light at first, don't panic — it oxidizes (darkens slightly) within 10–15 minutes to match your skin tone.
  • A damp beauty sponge is the best tool for applying any base for combination skin — it sheers out the product and prevents overloading oily areas.
  • Blush placement tip: for the most natural look, smile and apply to the apples of the cheeks, then blend up toward the temples with a fluffy brush.
  • Set a 2-hour reminder to reapply Bening's SPF spray over your makeup — sun protection doesn't stop once makeup is on!
