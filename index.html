Know Your Skin Combination Skin Type

✦ What is Combination Skin?

Combination skin is characterised by having both oily and dry areas on the same face. Typically, the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) tends to be oilier and more prone to enlarged pores and breakouts, while the cheeks and outer areas can be normal to dry. This requires a balanced approach — targeting excess oil without stripping moisture.

  • T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) is oily, shiny, and prone to clogged pores
  • Cheeks and outer face may feel dry, tight, or normal
  • Pores tend to be more visible on the nose and chin areas
  • Can experience both breakouts and dry patches simultaneously
  • Skin texture and needs can change with seasons, hormones, and stress
💧

Hydration is Key

Even with an oily T-zone, combination skin still needs consistent hydration. Skipping moisturiser can trigger excess oil production as the skin tries to compensate for dehydration.

🔬

Low pH Cleansers

Always use low-pH cleansers (pH 4.5–5.5) to maintain your skin's natural acid mantle. High pH cleansers disrupt the skin barrier, leading to dryness on cheeks and more oil on the T-zone.

☁️

Lightweight Formulas

Opt for gel or gel-cream moisturisers rather than heavy creams. These hydrate without overwhelming the oilier zones while still nourishing drier areas.

🛡️

Barrier Repair

Ceramides and niacinamide are your best friends. Strengthening the skin barrier helps regulate sebum production, reduce sensitivity, and even out skin texture across the face.

☀️

SPF is Non-Negotiable

UV exposure worsens oiliness, dryness, and uneven texture. Daily sunscreen prevents photo-ageing, hyperpigmentation, and keeps your skincare results lasting longer.

✦

Ingredients to Love

Centella asiatica, snail mucin, hyaluronic acid, ceramides, niacinamide, and low-pH actives work beautifully for combination skin — calming, repairing, and hydrating without clogging pores.

⚠️ Ingredients & Habits to Avoid

  • Avoid harsh foaming cleansers with high pH — they strip the skin barrier
  • Skip heavy, occlusive oils on the T-zone (use them only on dry cheek areas if needed)
  • Don't over-exfoliate — 1–2x per week maximum for combination skin
  • Avoid alcohol-based toners that dry out and irritate the skin
  • Never skip moisturiser even on oily days — dehydration triggers more sebum

Daily RitualMorning Skincare Routine

🌤️

Morning Routine

Cleanse → Treat → Protect · Total time: approx. 10–15 minutes

1
Cleanse — Choose Your Morning Low pH Cleanser
In the morning, your skin doesn't need a deep cleanse — just a gentle refresh. Pick based on how skin feels when you wake up:

🌿 Somethinc Low PH Gentle Cleanser — best for most mornings; lightweight, efficient, and barrier-safe for everyday use.
💙 Pond's Biome Cera-Hyamino Cleanser — best when skin feels dry, tight, or sensitive overnight; ceramides and hyaluronic acid make cleansing feel nourishing rather than stripping.
🐌 SOME BY MI Snail Truecica Gel Cleanser — use on mornings after a heavy sweat or a breakout-prone night; snail filtrate and centella give added repair benefits right from the first step.
💡 On very calm, dry-skin mornings a simple rinse with cool water is enough — save your cleanser for when skin actually needs it.
⏱ 60 seconds
2
Tone — SOME BY MI Snail Truecica Toner
Immediately after patting skin dry, apply the toner while skin is still slightly damp for maximum absorption. This step rebalances your skin's pH after cleansing and primes it to soak up the ampoule more effectively. Press into skin with both palms rather than swiping for a more nourishing application.
⏱ 30–60 seconds · let absorb
3

✦ Why Proper Makeup Removal Matters

Sleeping in makeup is one of the fastest ways to damage combination skin. Foundation, SPF, and setting products form an occlusive layer that traps sebum, dead cells, and environmental pollutants deep in pores — leading to congestion, breakouts, and accelerated skin ageing. Thorough makeup removal is the very first step of your evening skincare ritual, and it sets the foundation for every product that follows to actually absorb and work properly.

  • Makeup left overnight clogs pores and triggers breakouts on the T-zone
  • SPF and setting mists are occlusive — they must be fully removed for skin to breathe at night
  • Eye makeup residue causes lash breakage and eye irritation over time
  • A clean base helps toner and ampoule absorb up to 40% more effectively
  • Two-step removal (micellar + cleanser) is always more thorough than one step alone

  • Going to bed without removing lip products — lip stain stains lip skin over time
