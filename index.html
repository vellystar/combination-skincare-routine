My Combination Skin

Understanding your unique skin type is the foundation of every effective skincare routine. Here's everything to know about combination skin and how it behaves.

What is Combination Skin?

Combination skin features both oily and dry zones simultaneously — the T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) overproduces sebum while cheeks remain normal to dry, requiring a multi-zone approach to every routine step.

🧬

How It Works

Sebaceous glands are hyperactive along the T-zone, producing excess sebum. Cheeks have fewer active glands, leading to dryness or dehydration. Hormones, humidity, diet, and stress all shift this balance daily.

🌡️

How It Feels

Forehead and nose feel greasy by midday. Cheeks may feel tight or rough. Pores appear enlarged around the nose. Skin feels balanced right after cleansing but quickly reverts to its characteristic zones.

💧

Dehydration vs. Oiliness

Combination skin is often dehydrated — oily zones overproduce oil to compensate for lack of water moisture. This is why lightweight water-based hydration is essential; avoiding moisturizer entirely worsens oiliness.

🌦️

Indonesian Climate Effect

In humid tropical weather, the T-zone gets significantly oilier. In air-conditioned rooms, cheeks dry out faster. Adjust routine intensity accordingly and always prioritize SPF against Indonesia's intense equatorial UV.

✅ Look For in Products

  • Low pH cleansers (4.5–5.5) to maintain acid mantle
  • Lightweight, water-based moisturizers
  • Non-comedogenic formulas for T-zone
  • Niacinamide to regulate sebum production
  • Ceramides to repair barrier on dry cheeks
  • Centella asiatica for calming inflammation
  • Gel or gel-cream textures on oily zones
  • SPF every single morning — non-negotiable

❌ Things to Avoid

  • Harsh stripping cleansers (SLS/SLES-heavy)
  • Heavy occlusive creams all over T-zone
  • Alcohol-heavy toners or astringents
  • Over-exfoliating (disrupts the barrier)
  • Skipping moisturizer on oily zones
  • Mixing too many actives without patch testing
  • Touching your face frequently
  • Sleeping with makeup on
💡
Pro Tip — Indonesian Climate: Living in a hot, humid environment means T-zone will produce more sebum. Focus on oil control during the day with a mattifying sunscreen or setting powder on T-zone, while keeping cheeks hydrated. Always wear SPF — UV rays break down collagen and worsen hyperpigmentation significantly over time.

☀️ Midday Reapplication

SPF loses efficacy after 2 hours of direct sun exposure. Midday reapplication is non-negotiable in Indonesia's intense tropical climate.

⚡
Why Reapply? UV filters chemically break down after ~2 hours of sun exposure. Skipping reapplication cancels out morning SPF and allows UV damage to accelerate pigmentation, premature aging, and barrier damage — undoing all your skincare investment.

☁️ Mostly Indoors

Reapply once around noon with Bening's Spray. A 3-second spritz is sufficient. UV still penetrates glass windows and blue light from screens contributes to pigmentation over time.

🌞 Outdoor Activities

Reapply every 2 hours of direct sun. Blot T-zone first with Clean & Clear Oil Control Film, then spray Bening's generously from 15–20 cm in a slow sweep. Press lightly with fingertips. Top with translucent powder to mattify T-zone.

💦 After Sweating

Pat skin dry gently then reapply sunscreen spray — the spray format is far less disruptive to makeup than cream formulas. Follow with translucent powder for a fresh, set finish.

🎨 Over Full Makeup

Spray Bening's at arm's length, let settle 30 seconds, press gently with a beauty sponge — never rub. Finish with translucent powder and setting mist to lock everything back in.

What It Looks Like
An egg-shaped foam sponge — the pointed tip for precise areas (under eyes, around nose), the rounded base for larger areas (cheeks, forehead). Use damp for a skin-like finish, dry for more coverage.
What It Does
Blends liquid and cream makeup into the skin for the most natural, skin-like finish possible — better than fingers for most foundations and far better than brushes for cushions. The bouncing application motion presses product into skin rather than dragging it, preventing streaks and patchiness on dry cheek areas.
How to Use
Always dampen first: Wet the sponge, squeeze out all excess water so it's damp but not dripping. This expands the foam so it absorbs less product. Bounce, never drag: Use a rapid stippling / bouncing motion — press firmly for more coverage, lightly for a sheerer finish. Pointed tip: Blend concealer under eyes, around nose and mouth. Midday reapplication: After Bening's sunscreen spray settles, press gently with the sponge to help the SPF absorb without disturbing makeup.

General Brush Care Rules:

🔹 Wash eye brushes at least once a week — bacteria and oils from the eye area transfer quickly.
🔹 Wash face brushes every 2–3 uses for liquid products, once a week for powder.
🔹 Never soak the ferrule (the metal band) — water loosens the glue and bristles fall out.
🔹 Reshape while wet — pinch bristles back into shape before drying.
🔹 Dry flat or bristles-down — never upright in a cup, as water runs into the ferrule.
🔹 Dedicated brushes per product — avoid using the same brush for both light and dark shades without cleaning first, as residue transfers and muddies new colours.
🔹 Spot clean between uses with a dry brush cleaner spray for quick colour switching during a full makeup session.
